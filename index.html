pay attention to Child malnutrition

Child malnutrition estimates for the indicators stunting, wasting, overweight and underweight describe the magnitude and patterns of under- and overnutrition. UNICEF-WHO-WB Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates inter-agency group updates regularly the global and regional estimates in prevalence and numbers for each indicator. In 2018, globally there were 149 million children under 5 year of age were stunted, 49 million wasted and 40 million overweight.

In this project, we used these tools to build some interactive data visualizations which can efficiently provide necessary information for people to learn about the situation.

Visulation

Now let's besign our visulation trip.

This dashboard includes two bar chart, a intuitive visualization about the change of number and proportion of global malnourished children over time. Which shows a clear trend.

This is a line chart with different colors representing different countries. It is mainly used to describe the trend of the proportion of malnourished children in different countries over time, and could be used to compare data between any or more countries.

This is also a line chart, with different colors representing different countries, which is mainly used to describe the trend of the proportion of malnourished children in different regions over time. You can clearly see which region has the largest data at any time, and the trend of change in any region could also be viewed easily.

This dashboard generally describes the prevalence caused by stunting, development trends in various regions, and influencing factors.
